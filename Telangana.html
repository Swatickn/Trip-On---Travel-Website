TELANGANA

From Golconda’s Echoes to Charminar’s Charm

01

Ramoji Film City, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, is the world’s largest integrated film city and a dream destination for cinema lovers. Spread over a sprawling 2,000 acres, it offers a fascinating glimpse into the world of filmmaking while serving as a vibrant tourist attraction. The complex boasts stunning sets, beautifully landscaped gardens, and themed zones that make it a paradise for both filmmakers and visitors.
Established by Ramoji Rao in 1991, this iconic film city has hosted the production of countless movies in various languages, making it a hub for the Indian film industry. Visitors can explore grand sets replicating foreign locales, ancient palaces, bustling streets, and even fantasy worlds. The facility is also equipped with state-of-the-art studios, post-production units, and technical infrastructure, making it a one-stop solution for film production.
Apart from its cinematic charm, Ramoji Film City offers numerous attractions for tourists, including live shows, adventure activities, amusement rides, and themed gardens. The place is family-friendly, with something for all age groups, from thrilling stunt shows to tranquil butterfly gardens. The guided tours here make sure you don’t miss any of the iconic sets or behind-the-scenes magic.
For those seeking an immersive experience, Ramoji Film City also offers resort stays, allowing visitors to enjoy its charm over multiple days. Whether you’re a movie buff, a family on vacation, or simply curious about the magic of cinema, this destination guarantees entertainment, learning, and unforgettable memories.

Best time to visit: October to March

02

Charminar, located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana, is an iconic monument and one of India’s most recognized landmarks. Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it stands as a symbol of the city’s rich history and vibrant culture. The structure’s name, meaning “Four Minarets,” refers to its four grand towers, each offering a glimpse into the grandeur of Indo-Islamic architecture.
The monument is constructed from granite and lime mortar, featuring intricate stucco work and delicate floral motifs that showcase the craftsmanship of the Qutb Shahi era. Standing 56 meters tall, Charminar is not only a historical masterpiece but also a bustling hub surrounded by lively markets, making it a blend of heritage and everyday life.
One of the most famous attractions around Charminar is the Laad Bazaar, known for its dazzling bangles, pearls, and traditional Hyderabadi jewelry. The area is also a food lover’s paradise, with numerous stalls offering authentic local delicacies such as Irani chai, biryani, and kebabs. The evening lights around Charminar further enhance its charm, creating a magical atmosphere.

Best time to visit: October to March

03

Golconda Fort, located about 11 km from Hyderabad, Telangana, is a grand historical citadel that once served as the capital of the medieval Golconda Sultanate. Built in the 13th century by the Kakatiya dynasty and later expanded by the Qutb Shahi rulers, the fort is renowned for its architectural brilliance, strategic planning, and rich history. It was once the heart of a flourishing diamond trade, producing some of the world’s most famous gems, including the Koh-i-Noor and the Hope Diamond.
The fort is an architectural marvel, with massive granite walls stretching up to 11 km in circumference and rising as high as 15 to 18 meters. Its unique acoustic system is particularly fascinating — a clap at the entrance can be heard clearly at the highest point of the fort, an ingenious feature used for communication and security in the past. Visitors can also explore the fort’s royal palaces, audience halls, temples, and intricate water supply system.
One of the major highlights for tourists is the evening light and sound show, which narrates the fort’s glorious past with captivating storytelling, visuals, and music. The panoramic view of Hyderabad from the top of the fort is breathtaking, especially at sunset when the city glows under the fading light.
Today, Golconda Fort stands as a reminder of the grandeur and cultural richness of the Deccan region, attracting historians, photographers, and curious travelers alike. It is not just a fort but a living chapter of India’s medieval legacy.

Best time to visit: October to February

04

Hussain Sagar Lake, located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana, is a sprawling, heart-shaped artificial lake that beautifully connects the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad. Built in 1563 by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah, it was originally constructed to meet the city’s water and irrigation needs. Over the centuries, it has evolved into one of Hyderabad’s most popular leisure and tourist spots, offering scenic beauty and cultural charm.
One of the lake’s most iconic features is the massive monolithic statue of Gautama Buddha, which stands on Gibraltar Rock at the center of the lake. This statue, carved out of a single piece of white granite, adds to the lake’s serene and spiritual ambiance. Visitors can reach it via boat rides, which also provide stunning views of the lake and the city skyline.
Surrounding the lake are several attractions, including Lumbini Park, Necklace Road, and Tank Bund, making it a hub for evening strolls, picnics, and photography. The area comes alive after sunset with twinkling city lights reflecting in the water, creating a magical atmosphere. Water sports and recreational activities, such as boating, parasailing, and jet skiing, further enhance the experience.
Steeped in history and surrounded by urban vibrance, Hussain Sagar Lake serves as both a peaceful retreat and a lively gathering place, blending Hyderabad’s heritage with its modern lifestyle.

Best time to visit: October to March

05

Chowmahalla Palace, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, is a magnificent architectural gem that once served as the official residence of the Nizams, the rulers of Hyderabad. The name “Chowmahalla” translates to “Four Palaces,” reflecting the complex’s layout of four distinct palaces within its grounds. Built in the 18th century during the reign of Nizam Salabat Jung, the palace is renowned for its opulent architecture, intricate craftsmanship, and grandeur that mirrors the royal lifestyle of the Nizams.
The palace complex showcases a stunning blend of Persian, Indo-Saracenic, and European architectural styles. Its most striking structure is the Khilwat Mubarak, the grand Durbar Hall adorned with spectacular Belgian crystal chandeliers, marble flooring, and intricately carved arches. This hall was historically used for ceremonial gatherings and important state functions.
Sprawling gardens, fountains, and courtyards add to the serene charm of the palace. The complex also houses a fascinating collection of vintage cars, royal artifacts, costumes, and weaponry, offering visitors a glimpse into Hyderabad’s regal past.
Today, Chowmahalla Palace stands as a beautifully preserved heritage site, often hosting cultural events, exhibitions, and even private functions. Its grandeur and elegance make it a must-visit destination for history lovers, architecture enthusiasts, and photographers alike.

Best time to visit: October to March

06

Nehru Zoological Park, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, is one of India’s largest and most well-maintained zoos, covering an area of about 380 acres. Established in 1963 and named after India’s first Prime Minister, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, the park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, families, and wildlife enthusiasts.
The zoo houses over 1,500 animals, birds, and reptiles belonging to more than 200 species. Notable inhabitants include tigers, lions, leopards, Indian elephants, rhinoceroses, and various exotic species such as chimpanzees and jaguars. Bird lovers can admire colorful macaws, flamingos, pelicans, and many native and migratory birds. The reptile house showcases snakes, crocodiles, and other fascinating reptiles.
One of the park’s highlights is its safari rides, such as the lion safari, tiger safari, and bear safari, which allow visitors to observe animals in large, open enclosures that closely resemble their natural habitats. The zoo also features a nocturnal animal house, butterfly park, aquarium, and a miniature train ride, making it an engaging experience for children.

Best time to visit: October to March

07

NTR Gardens, located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana, is a beautifully landscaped urban park spread over 36 acres, offering a refreshing escape from the city’s hustle and bustle. Built in memory of Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR), a legendary Telugu actor and former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, the park combines greenery, leisure, and recreation in one spot.
The gardens feature manicured lawns, vibrant flower beds, and walking paths, making it a popular destination for families, couples, and tourists. There are several attractions within the park, including a Japanese garden, a desert garden, and a large fountain area. Children especially enjoy the play zones, toy train rides, and boating facilities in the small lake inside the park.
A highlight for many visitors is the giant Machan Tree (tree-top restaurant), offering a unique dining experience, and the futuristic fountains, which look especially beautiful at night when illuminated. The park is also located close to other key attractions such as Hussain Sagar Lake, Lumbini Park, and Birla Mandir, making it an easy addition to a city tour.

08

The Qutb Shahi Tombs, located near the Golconda Fort in Hyderabad, Telangana, are a stunning collection of mausoleums built by the rulers of the Qutb Shahi dynasty (1518–1687). These tombs are among the oldest historical monuments in Hyderabad and represent a blend of Persian, Pathan, and Hindu architectural styles.
Set in landscaped gardens, each tomb is built on a raised platform and topped with domes, adorned with intricately carved stonework. The complex houses the resting places of all seven Qutb Shahi rulers, along with family members, nobles, and other important figures of the era.
Originally constructed with beautiful stucco decorations and surrounded by minarets, the tombs have been partially restored, and their charm remains intact. The surrounding gardens, known as Ibrahim Bagh, add to the peaceful and regal atmosphere.
Today, the Qutb Shahi Tombs stand as a testament to Hyderabad’s royal past and its architectural brilliance. Visitors often explore the site in combination with a trip to Golconda Fort, as together they narrate the story of the Qutb Shahi dynasty’s rise and glory. Early mornings or late afternoons are the best times to visit, when the sunlight enhances the intricate details of the monuments.

Best time to visit: -----

09

Shamirpet Lake, located about 24 km from Hyderabad in Telangana, is a serene artificial lake built during the Nizam’s reign. Surrounded by rocky terrain and lush greenery, the lake is a popular getaway for those seeking peace away from the city’s rush. It is also known for its calm waters that beautifully reflect the sunrise and sunset, making it a favorite spot for photography enthusiasts and couples. The area around the lake is dotted with small cottages and resorts, offering visitors the chance to spend a relaxed weekend close to nature.
The lake is a paradise for bird lovers, as it attracts a wide variety of migratory and resident bird species, especially during winter. Spotting pelicans, herons, and painted storks is common here, making it an excellent destination for birdwatching. The peaceful atmosphere, combined with the occasional calls of birds, adds to the lake’s charm.

Best time to visit: November to February

10

Kasu Brahmananda Reddy National Park, located in the heart of Hyderabad, Telangana, is a lush green oasis spread over 390 acres. Also known as KBR National Park, it is named after the former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh, Kasu Brahmananda Reddy. The park is a perfect blend of nature and heritage, as it also houses the historic Chiran Palace. Its sprawling landscapes provide a refreshing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, making it a popular spot for walkers, joggers, and nature enthusiasts.
The park boasts a rich variety of flora, with dense forests, open grasslands, and seasonal flowering plants that add color to every corner. Several species of medicinal plants can be found here, along with a mix of native and exotic trees. The serene walking trails lined with shady trees make it an ideal place for morning and evening strolls, offering visitors a calm and rejuvenating experience.
Wildlife lovers can also spot a diverse range of fauna in the park. It is home to over 100 species of birds, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. Small mammals like pangolins, porcupines, and monitor lizards inhabit the park, while butterflies flutter around during the blooming seasons. The peaceful environment combined with the occasional chirping of birds creates a perfect setting for photography and relaxation.

Best time to visit: October to March
